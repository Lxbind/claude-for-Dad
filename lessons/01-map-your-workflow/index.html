Lesson 01

Map your workflow

~15 min fill in your actual tasks saves automatically

By the end you'll have a chart of your real work that we'll use as the textbook for every lesson after this. The chart is more important than any AI tool you learn. If we don't know what your job looks like, we can't tell you which tools fit it.

This is the one lesson I want you to do slowly, Dad. Every "AI tip" you'll ever hear from now on either fits this chart or it doesn't — and that saves you months of chasing shiny things. — L.

How to do it

  1. List 8–12 tasks you do at work. Not the official job description — the actual stuff. Recurring meetings, reports you produce, fires you put out, requests you field.
  2. Include the boring recurring stuff. "Renewals that nobody tracks." "Updating the supplier list when someone changes departments." Those are often the highest-leverage targets for AI later.
  3. Be specific about who receives what. "Memo for R&D dept lead" beats "report."
  4. Don't over-edit. If a task is messy, type it messy. Lesson 2 onwards uses this chart to figure out which AI tools fit. Wrong-but-real beats clean-but-fake.

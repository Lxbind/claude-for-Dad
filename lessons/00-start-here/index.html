The four ideas

1. What an LLM actually is

What is a large language model (LLM) actually doing when you chat with it?

  • Searching the internet for the best answer
  • Predicting the next word, over and over, based on patterns from training
  • Looking up facts in a database
  • Running code to compute answers

Why this matters: because an LLM is predicting, it's brilliant at organizing what you give it and weaker at recalling specific facts. Lean on it for synthesis, structure, drafting. Verify it on numbers, dates, names.

2. What "a chat" actually is

When you open a new chat with Claude, what does Claude know about you?

  • Everything from your past chats
  • Your files, calendar, and email
  • Nothing — every new chat starts blank
  • Whatever was in the news up to yesterday

Why this matters: if you want Claude to know something about your work, you have to either tell it (paste it into the chat), save it (use a Project), or automate it (use a skill). Otherwise, you're starting from zero every time.

3. Why surfaces exist

Claude has multiple "surfaces" — claude.ai (web chat), Claude Code (terminal), Claude Desktop (with app connections), etc. Why?

  • Each surface uses a different, smarter version of Claude
  • The model is the same; each surface has different access to your files / apps / context
  • Each surface is specialized for one kind of task
  • It's a sales strategy — Anthropic wants you on multiple products

Why this matters: the right answer to "how do I do X with Claude?" almost always starts with "first, pick the right surface." Lessons 4 and 5 are entirely about this.

4. What this means for you

Given the three things above, what's the smartest way to learn Claude for your job?

  • Read all of Anthropic's docs cover to cover
  • Watch as many YouTube demos as possible
  • Map your actual job first, then learn just the AI tools that fit it
  • Just start using claude.ai and figure it out by trial and error

The curriculum, in full

8 lessons≈ 2h 35m total

The eight lessons in order, with a paragraph on why each one lives where it does. If you're wondering whether to skip ahead — read the "why" for the lesson you want to jump to. Usually it says "no, you need the one before this first."

Design principles

  1. Teach by doing. No lesson is a vocabulary lecture. Every lesson is interactive — pick answers, fill forms, run real prompts.
  2. 15–30 min hard cap. If it doesn't fit, split it.
  3. Pick-and-learn over read-and-hope. Multiple choice with feedback is how the content gets in. Reading-only sections stay short.
  4. Demo first, name later. You use the thing (or see it used) before you learn its formal name. Vocabulary attaches to lived experience.
  5. Your work, not generic examples. Lesson 1's workflow chart is the textbook for every lesson after it.
  6. Zero installs for as long as possible. Lessons 0–5 are pure browser. Lesson 6 uses claude.ai (free signup). Lesson 7 is the first to touch Claude Code.

What we deliberately skip

  • No "what is a transformer" or other model-internals material. Doesn't help you get work done.
  • No prompt-engineering theory. Good prompts are demonstrated, not taught as a subject.
  • No tool-name memorization quizzes. If you don't remember which tool is which after Lesson 4, you'll re-derive it from your workflow in Lesson 5. The work is the textbook, not the words.
  • No confidentiality mechanics yet. Important but separate — we'll address it before you put any Sanofi-internal data into a chat.

The eight lessons

  1. 00 Start here The four load-bearing ideas — LLMs predict, chats start blank, surfaces differ by access, your work is the textbook. Without these, every later lesson misfires. 10 min · 4 quizzes
  2. 01 Map your workflow Fill in your actual procurement tasks. Saves in your browser, exports as markdown. This chart is the diagnostic — every lesson after this refers back to it. 15 min · Form
  3. 02 AI in your workflow Five worked procurement scenarios — drafting, synthesis, contracts with Claude Code, inbox with MCP, calendar with MCP. You see each tool in action before you learn its name. 20 min · 5 scenarios
  4. 03 Where everything lives The folder map nobody teaches. Where skills go, where settings live, the two-worlds rule (local disk vs. Anthropic cloud). Cheat sheet at the end. 20 min · Visual + 4 quizzes
  5. 04 The AI tool zoo One quiz card per Claude surface — claude.ai, Claude Code, Skills, MCP, Projects, Desktop. The vocabulary lesson, except the names now have hooks because you've already met them. 20 min · 6 quizzes
  6. 05 Match tools to your work Practice quizzes on example procurement tasks, then apply the decision rule to your own chart. The patterns stop being abstract here. 15 min · 6 quizzes
  7. 06 Try Claude on a real task Pick the row from your workflow where Claude can most obviously help. Walk through actually doing it on claude.ai. First real productivity win. 25 min · Hands-on
  8. 07 Build your first skill In-browser skill builder — fill in name, description, instructions. Download the resulting SKILL.md and install it. First reusable piece of your own AI workflow. 30 min · Builder

Done criteria

You're done when you can:

  • Name what claude.ai / Claude Code / a skill / an MCP server are, without checking.
  • Point at any file Claude touches and say where it lives.
  • Look at one of your procurement tasks and pick the right tool for it.
  • Show me you've used Claude on a real task once.
  • Point at one skill you shipped that fits your job.

The course doesn't go further than that on purpose. Past this point, learning more is just doing more.
